Explain Anthony Ortony's model of emotional design -> Anthony Ortony's model of emotional design, known as the OCC model, explains how emotions are generated through the evaluation or appraisal of events, agents, and objects. It categorizes emotions into three types: event-based, agent-based, and object-based. The model suggests that emotions arise based on how individuals assess the outcomes of events, the actions of others, or their attraction to objects.
