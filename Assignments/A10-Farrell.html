Question 1



The original prototype of the Palm Pilot, known as the "Palm Taxi," was actually a block of wood!



The Co-Founder used this simple wooden block as a stand-in for the device to simulate how people might use a portable digital assistant. He wanted to determine if a handheld device for organizing information would feel comfortable and practical in daily use

Question 2



A product prototype is a tangible model of a physical or digital item created to simulate its appearance, functionality, and usability, allowing developers to test and refine the product before production. This type of prototype focuses on evaluating aspects like ergonomics, features, and design



A service prototype is less about a physical object and more about testing interactions within a service experience. It focuses on simulating and evaluating the steps of a service, often through role-playing or scripted scenarios

Question 4



High-fidelity prototypes are highly detailed, interactive models of a product that closely resemble the final design and user experience. These prototypes include realistic visuals, interactive elements, animations, and accurate content



One key disadvantage of high-fidelity prototypes is that they can be time-consuming and resource-intensive to create, which can slow down the design process.

Microsoft's Inclusive Design guide on "Respecting Focus" highlights how to create respectful and accessible user experiences by minimizing unnecessary distractions and preserving user focus.



It emphasizes techniques like reducing unexpected sounds or pop-ups, prioritizing relevant content, and providing clear paths for interaction. The guide encourages designers to be mindful of diverse attention spans and cognitive needs, fostering experiences that are engaging without overwhelming.
